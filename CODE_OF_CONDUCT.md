# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Score247 is a community project built around a sport that brings people together across streets, fields, and rooftops. Everyone who contributes — regardless of experience, background, language, or where they learned to play — deserves to feel welcome here.

We are committed to maintaining a space that is respectful, collaborative, and free from harassment.

---

## Our Standards

**We expect contributors to:**

- Be respectful and constructive in all discussions
- Give and receive feedback gracefully
- Assume good intent before assuming bad faith
- Focus on the work, not the person
- Credit others for their ideas and contributions

**We do not tolerate:**

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks of any kind
- Discrimination based on gender, age, nationality, religion, ethnicity, or background
- Spam, self-promotion unrelated to the project, or bad-faith contributions
- Removing or falsifying author credits (this is also a license violation — see [LICENSE.md](LICENSE.md))

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces: GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, commit messages, and any other communication related to Score247.

---

## Enforcement

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:

1. A private or public warning
2. Temporary suspension from contributing
3. Permanent ban from the project

The project maintainer (waleedahmedja) has final authority on enforcement decisions.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it by opening a private GitHub Issue or contacting the maintainer directly.

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality.

---

## The Spirit of It

Score247 is about gully cricket — a sport that has always been played without referees, without stadiums, and without rules set from above. The community sets the norms.

That spirit extends here. Treat contributors the way you'd treat the guy who just joined your match. You don't know them yet, but they love the game — that's enough.

— **waleedahmedja**
